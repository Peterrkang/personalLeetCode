System Design Curriculum — Week by Week

Mid-level: design end-to-end & justify choices. Senior: deeper tradeoffs, scale numbers, failure modes, consistency — and drive the conversation.

The framework (use it every single time):
  1. Clarify requirements — functional + non-functional (scale, latency, consistency, availability).
  2. Estimate — QPS, storage, bandwidth (back-of-envelope).
  3. API design — define the core endpoints.
  4. High-level design — boxes & arrows: clients → LB → services → DB/cache/queue.
  5. Deep dive — pick 1–2 components, go deep (schema, sharding, algorithm).
  6. Bottlenecks & tradeoffs — what breaks at 10×? How do you scale/recover?
Spend ~90 min/day. Half the days = a concept; half = a case study. Always draw it (paper or Excalidraw) and say it out loud.

Phase 1 — Building blocks (Weeks 1–4)

Week 1 — Fundamentals

  • Latency vs throughput; availability (the "nines"); reliability vs availability.
  • Vertical vs horizontal scaling; stateless vs stateful services.
  • The numbers every engineer should know (latency table: memory vs SSD vs network).
  • Back-of-envelope estimation: QPS, storage, bandwidth.
  • Exercise: Estimate storage/QPS for a service with 100M DAU posting 2 items/day.

Week 2 — Networking & APIs

  • DNS, how a request travels; HTTP/HTTPS, HTTP/2 & 3 basics.
  • Load balancers: L4 vs L7, algorithms (round-robin, least-conn, hashing).
  • REST API design, idempotency, pagination, versioning, status codes.
  • gRPC vs REST vs GraphQL — when each.
  • Exercise: Design a clean REST API for a pastebin (CRUD + expiry).

Week 3 — Databases

  • SQL vs NoSQL — data models, when to pick which.
  • Indexing (B-tree), query optimization basics.
  • ACID vs BASE; transactions & isolation levels.
  • Normalization vs denormalization.
  • Exercise: Schema design for an e-commerce orders system; pick & justify the DB.

Week 4 — Caching & content delivery

  • Cache strategies: cache-aside, write-through, write-back, write-around.
  • Eviction policies (LRU/LFU); cache invalidation; TTLs.
  • CDNs — push vs pull, edge caching.
  • Replication: leader-follower, multi-leader; read replicas.
  • Exercise: Add a caching + CDN layer to last week's e-commerce design; discuss invalidation.

Phase 2 — Scaling & case studies (Weeks 5–8)

Week 5 — Partitioning & sharding

  • Horizontal partitioning (sharding) strategies: range, hash, directory.
  • Consistent hashing — understand it cold; can you draw the ring?
  • Hotspots, rebalancing, the "celebrity problem."
  • Replication + sharding combined.
  • Exercise: Shard a 10TB user table; handle a viral user.

Week 6 — Async processing & messaging

  • Message queues (SQS, RabbitMQ) vs streaming (Kafka).
  • Pub/sub; producer/consumer; at-least-once vs exactly-once delivery.
  • Backpressure, dead-letter queues, idempotent consumers.
  • Batch vs stream processing.
  • Exercise: Design an async image-processing pipeline (upload → resize → notify).

Week 7 — Case studies: foundational

  • URL shortener (TinyURL): key generation, collision handling, redirect path, analytics, cache.
  • Rate limiter: token bucket vs leaky bucket vs sliding window; distributed rate limiting with Redis.
  • Exercise: Do each end-to-end on paper using the 6-step framework. Time yourself: 45 min each.

Week 8 — Case studies: feeds

  • News feed / Twitter timeline: fan-out on write vs read, the celebrity problem, feed ranking, caching.
  • Instagram-style photo sharing: storage, CDN, metadata DB.
  • Exercise: Twitter design in 45 min, then critique your own design for bottlenecks at 10×.

Phase 3 — Deep dives & senior signal (Weeks 9–13)

Week 9 — Case studies: real-time

  • Chat system (WhatsApp/Messenger): WebSockets, online presence, message delivery & ordering, group chat, read receipts.
  • Notification system: fan-out, third-party (APNs/FCM), retries, dedup.
  • Exercise: Chat system in 45 min; deep-dive on message delivery guarantees.

Week 10 — Case studies: media & storage

  • YouTube / Netflix: video upload pipeline, transcoding, CDN, adaptive bitrate streaming, recommendations at a high level.
  • Distributed file/object storage (S3, Dropbox): chunking, dedup, metadata service, consistency.
  • Exercise: Design YouTube; deep-dive on the transcoding pipeline.

Week 11 — Case studies: location & search

  • Uber / Lyft: geospatial indexing (geohash, quadtree), matching riders/drivers, real-time location updates, surge.
  • Search / typeahead (autocomplete): trie at scale, ranking, sharding the trie, caching prefixes.
  • Exercise: Design typeahead; deep-dive on trie sharding & top-K.

Week 12 — Advanced theory & senior depth

  • CAP theorem and PACELC — applied, not memorized.
  • Consistency models: strong, eventual, causal, read-your-writes.
  • Consensus: Raft/Paxos conceptually; leader election; quorums.
  • Observability: metrics, logging, tracing, alerting, SLAs/SLOs/SLIs.
  • Failure handling: circuit breakers, retries w/ backoff + jitter, bulkheads, graceful degradation.
  • Exercise: Take any prior design and add: monitoring, failure recovery, and a consistency-model justification.

Week 13 — Mock week

  • 2 full design interviews (45–60 min each) with a peer or interviewing.io.
  • Re-do your 2 weakest case studies cold.
  • Prepare 3–4 deep-dive stories about systems you've actually built (huge for senior).

Senior-level checklist (what separates mid from senior)

  • You drive the interview, not the interviewer.
  • You quantify: "at 50k QPS this DB tops out, so we shard by X."
  • You name explicit tradeoffs and pick one with a reason.
  • You discuss failure modes and recovery, not just the happy path.
  • You bring up consistency, idempotency, and data integrity unprompted.
  • You can zoom into any box and design it in detail.
  • You connect design to real systems you've operated.

Behavioral (don't neglect — start week 9)

Prepare 6–8 STAR stories: conflict, failure, leadership/influence, ambiguity, biggest impact, disagreement with manager, mentoring. Write them down. For senior, emphasize scope, influence, and ownership.
